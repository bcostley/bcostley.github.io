Floeter TH to Nehalem Divide TH

The Floeter trailhead is about 6 (estimate) miles up ScapV highway from the intersection of ScapV with HWY 47. This segement is mostly far enough away from the highway you might not notice it. It is though relentlessly up though at a moderate grade.

The trail is pretty in the fall.

Behind this sign is a short unofficial trail which goes down to the East Fork Nehalem where it is just a small stream.

<

Follow the stream/trail up a short bit to the west portal of an abandoned train tunnel. You can just see the light at the end of the tunnel.

These two cute little waterfalls straddle the tunnel portal.

This segment ends at the Nehalem Divide trailhead. This is the highest elevation on the trail. Beyond the divide you will be in the Columbia River watershed.
