Vernonia Mill Pond aka Vernonia Lake

The Vernonia Mill Pond (officially Vernonia Lake) was build for the Oregon-American Mill which was in operation in the early to mid-20th century in Vernonia. The mill closed down when the old growth was all cut. The mill pond and some structures, notably the walls of the fuel building, remain.

This is an easy paved walk around the pond. This is a good walk for birdwatching with bald eagles, osprey, herons, ducks, and redwinged blackbirds among others.

Blackberries line much of the path making for a treat in early fall. There is a swingset near the parking. Fishing is popular around the pond from the banks and from two docks.

In season, Calypso's vends ice cream, hamburgers, and hot dogs.

A primitive campground is adjacent to the path.

Webb Way connects the mill pond to Anderson Park near downtown Vernonia.
